mn6 fingerprint false positives — cross-analysis
=================================================

Fingerprint: fp = (c1 + c2*218) & $FF
16 false positives out of 17,576 possible 3-letter strings (0.09%).

Distance metric: weighted Hamming
  1.0 per differing character position.
  0.5 if the wrong key is QWERTY-adjacent to the right key (fat-finger).
  Range 0 (identical) .. 3 (all characters differ).

For each false positive, "H-slot" is the legal mnemonic it mimics via the
hash+fingerprint check.  "Nearest" is the closest of the 56 legal mnemonics
by weighted distance — which may differ from H-slot.

All 16 false positives differ from their H-slot in all 3 characters
(Hamming = 3).  Every false positive is at weighted distance >= 1.5 from
every legal mnemonic — no false positive can be reached by a single
adjacent-key error from any legal mnemonic.  Two independent errors are
required to produce any false positive from any legal mnemonic.

KYY analysis (most notable FP)
-------------------------------
KYY mimics SED and sits at wdist 1.5 from both INY and LDY:
  - wdist(KYY, INY) = 0.5 (K adj I) + 1.0 (Y vs N) + 0.0 = 1.5
  - wdist(KYY, LDY) = 0.5 (K adj L) + 1.0 (Y vs D) + 0.0 = 1.5
No single-key error from any legal mnemonic produces KYY.

Note on assembly carry behaviour
---------------------------------
The hash h = (c1*8 + c3*15 + T[c2]) & $3F requires two CLC instructions:

  1. CLC before ADC mn6_h_tmp   — SEC/SBC (c3*15) leaves carry undefined.
  2. CLC before ADC mn6_hash_t,Y — c1*8+c3*15 mod 256 may set carry when
                                   the true sum exceeds 255.

Both CLCs are necessary; without them, mnemonics with large c1 and c3
values receive wrong slot assignments.  See src/mn6.s.
